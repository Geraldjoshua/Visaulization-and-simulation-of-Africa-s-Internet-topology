Simulator User Manual

A. INTRODUCTION

This simulator has been built to simulate Africa's internet topology. The Simulator obtains its initial topology from the visualizer.

B. USE CASES

The following are the use cases that you achieve with the simulator.

  1. Add IXP Node.
  2. Delete Node.
  3. Add connection between two nodes.
  4. Delete connection between two nodes.
  5. Simulate internet traffic flow.
  6. View and record RTT associated with Source ASN - Destination ASN pair.

Add IXP:

  • Click on the “Add IXP Node” button.
  • A pop up form called “Add IXP Node” will open.
  • Input both Latitude and the Longitude of the IXP node you wish to add to the topology.
  • Click the green “Add IXP Node” button to finalize the process.
  • The added IXP node will appear on the topology.
  • Click the “Close” button to close the pop up form.

Delete Node:

  • Click on the “Delete Node” button.
  • A pop up form called “Delete Node” will open.
  • Input both Latitude and the Longitude of the IXP node you wish to remove from the topology.
  • Click the green “Delete Node” button to finalize the process.
  • The removed node will disappear from the topology and all its associated links.
  • Click the “Close” button to close the pop up form.

Add Connection:

  • Click on the “Add Connection” button.
  • A pop up form called “Add Connection” will open.
  • Input both locations for the source and the destination for the connection you wish to add to the topology.The format of the added source location should be: (Latitude,Longitude,Source ASN number) and  the added destination location should be: (Latitude,Longitude,Destination ASN number) . To aid this process, click on any ASN node on the graph to see the info windows that have this information. Eg (30.72, 29.77,8452) where 30.72 is the Latitude and 29.77 is the LOngitude and 8452 is the ASN number.
  • Click the green “Add Connection” button to finalize the process.
  • The added connection will appear on the topology.
  • Click the “Close” button to close the pop up form.

Remove Connection:

  • Click on the “Remove Connection” button.
  • A pop up form called “Remove Connection” will open.
  • Input both locations for the source and the destination of the connection you wish to remove to the topology. The format of the source location should be: (Latitude,Longitude,Source ASN number) and  the destination location should be: (Latitude,Longitude,Destination ASN number) . To aid this process, click on any ASN node on the graph to see the info windows that have this information. Eg (30.72, 29.77,8452) where 30.72 is the Latitude and 29.77 is the LOngitude and 8452 is the ASN number.
  • Click the green “Remove Connection” button to finalize the process.
  • The removed connection will disappear from the topology.
  • Click the “Close” button to close the pop up form.

Simulate Traffic and View RTT:

  • Click on the blue “Simulation” button.
  • A pop up form called “Simulation” will open.
  • Input both locations for the source and the destination for the internet traffic you wish to simulate on the topology. The format of the added source location should be: (Latitude,Longitude,Source ASN number) and  the added destination location should be: (Latitude,Longitude,Destination ASN number) . To aid this process, click on any ASN node on the graph to see the info windows that have this information. Eg (30.72, 29.77,8452) where 30.72 is the Latitude and 29.77 is the LOngitude and 8452 is the ASN number.
  • Click the green “Start Simulation” button to start simulation.
  • Simulation of the shortest route will be animated and shown.
  • The total Link delay will be shown and indicated.
  • Click the “Close” button to close the pop up form.
